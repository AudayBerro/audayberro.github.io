Welcome to My Personal Website

Hello and welcome to my corner of the internet! I am Auday Berro, a computer scientist specializing in natural language processing (NLP) and Dialogue Services (DS). My work is centered on the field of paraphrase generation (PG) and harnessing the potential of language models, including large language models (LLMs), to push the boundaries of what is possible in human-computer interactions.

About Me

I recently completed my Ph.D. in Computer Science at the Université Claude Bernard Lyon 1, where my research focused on improving training data acquisition for intent recognition in task-oriented DS. My work involved leveraging automated PG techniques to address the inherent variability and ambiguity in user utterances. Guided by the expertise of Prof. Boualem Benatallah, I explored advanced topics in NLP, machine learning and DS.

My Research

In the dynamic field of NLP, my research has focused on improving human-computer interaction through a better understanding of user requests in textual natural language. My PhD thesis, entitled “Paraphrase Generation for Conversational Services Learning”, investigates how automated PG can be used to create diverse and high-quality datasets, essential for training task-oriented DS. My focus was on pushing the boundaries of DS and making interactions with technology more intuitive and efficient. By tackling the challenges of PG and dataset quality, I contributed to the development of more robust intent recognition models for DS.

What I Do:

  • Natural Language Understanding: Innovating scalable and cost-effective methods for acquiring high-quality training data for task-oriented DS
  • Paraphrase Generation: levraging and developping techniques to reformulate user utterances into multiple variations while preserving their meaning, which is crucial for training robust DS
  • Data Quality and Diversity: Implementing and evaluating PG pipelines to ensure that generated datasets are both semantically relevant and diverse (lexical and syntactic diversity)

Key Contributions throughout my research:

  • Designed and evaluated a baseline PG pipeline
  • Developed a taxonomy of error types for transformer-based PG models
  • Dataset Construction and Model Development: Create a new manually annotated dataset for paraphrasing tasks and use it to develop a multilabel annotation model through fine-tuning a BERT language model.
  • Explored the potential of LLMs like GPT-3.5 Turbo for generating syntactically diverse paraphrases.

My research interests include:

  • Dialogue Services: training data acquisition, intent and entity recognition
  • Quality Control in Crowdsourcing and AI/LLM paraphrase generation
  • AI/crowd-based Training Data Curation and Quality
  • Prompt engineering and retrieval-augmented generation (RAG)
